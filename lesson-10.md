## THEORY — The Full SOLO Journey (10 minutes)

### Where You Started vs Where You Are Now

| SOLO Level | What it looks like | When you were here |
|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Pre-structural | "I don't know what computational thinking is" | Before Lesson 1 |
| Uni-structural | "I can write a program that scrolls text" | Lessons 1–2 |
| Multi-structural | "I can use buttons AND loops AND variables" | Lessons 3–5 |
| Relational | "I can combine sensors, variables and decisions in one system" | Lessons 6–7 |
| Extended Abstract | "I can design, build and debug any program to solve a new problem" | Lessons 8–10 |

You are now at **Extended Abstract** — you don't just know facts about coding. You can USE those skills to solve NEW problems you haven't seen before.

### The Four CT Pillars — Where They Appeared

| Pillar | Where you used it |
|--------|------------------|
| **Decomposition** | Planning the game (Lesson 7), planning your own project (Lesson 9) |
| **Pattern Recognition** | Spotting that `if/else` always follows the same pattern; recognising when a loop is needed |
| **Abstraction** | Using variables to store sensor data (not the raw sensor every time); ignoring unimportant details |
| **Algorithm Design** | Writing step-by-step programs in every lesson |

### Where These Skills Go Next

Computational thinking is not just for coding. You use it when you:
- **Plan a project** — break it into tasks (decomposition)
- **Follow a recipe** — a sequence of steps (algorithm)
- **Budget money** — use variables to track amounts
- **Troubleshoot anything** — the 5-step debug process works everywhere

## SHOWCASE — Present Your Project (15 minutes)

### Format

You will present your Lesson 9 project to a partner, small group, or the whole class. This is NOT a formal performance — it's a conversation.

### What to Cover (3–4 minutes per person)

1. **What does your project do?**  
   *Describe it in plain English.*

2. **Demo it**  
   *Run the program in the simulator or on the real device.*

3. **Explain the IPO**  
   *What is the input? What processing does it do? What is the output?*

4. **Which concepts did you use?**  
   *Point to a part of your code and explain what concept it uses.*

5. **What would you improve?**  
   *If you had one more lesson, what would you add or change?*

### Listening Partner Guide

While your partner presents, you must:
- Ask at least ONE question
- Give one piece of **positive feedback** (something that worked well)
- Give one **suggestion** (one idea for improvement)

Use this sentence structure:
- "I noticed that your program… which shows you understand…"
- "One thing I wondered was…"
- "If I were going to improve it, I might…"
